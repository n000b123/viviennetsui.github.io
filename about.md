## My growth

I'm currently a final year data science and economics student at NUS. 

I've always been fascinated with how economics uses intricate logic and appropriately simplified models to explain reality. I enjoyed applied mathematics as well. These reasons led me to choose my college major. 

During my time in uni, I learnt so much about data science, econometrics, micro/macroeconomic theory, machine learning, mathematics, and statistics - and I'm just at the tip of the knowledge iceberg. The courses I took opened my eyes to the boundless possibilities of using data to solve problems in economic contexts. 

I took design thinking in my 1st year and thoroughly enjoyed the module, eventually figuring I was more creative and innovative than I thought. Later on, I worked at a biotech start-up during summer, where I built statistical models to analyse data from bin sensors installed at train stations. I liked the idea of working in a lean, flexible team on an innovative product. These all laid the seeds for my decision to take a "gap year" in my 3rd year to work at a start-up in Sweden under NUS Overseas Colleges. 

I ended up working at a maritime software company (not really a start-up though), as a data engineer for commodities contract lifecycle management software. I picked up data engineering from scratch and worked extensively with Azure Databricks. It was my first time working with production-level software and of course made lots of mistakes (accidentally disabling the whole database...), but I must say the skills and exposure I got were invaluable. 

It was also when I found an interest in business and product strategy. The challenge in positioning the delicately crafted CLM product in a slow digital adapting industry was indeed real, leading me to think about market viability, product positioning and innovation, and so on. I brought these learnings to my coursework at KTH Sweden, where I crafted business pitches about a live music discovery platform and a financial tracking application for families.

After a year well spent in the Nordics, I came back to Singapore refreshed for my final year. I worked briefly on a digital transformation project for a special needs school, where I realised impact creation in the social service sector was meaningful yet felt stifling. Innovation was the missing element.

I also fulfilled my childhood dream of being an architect by reading a minor in cities ("urban planning"), which was a pretty different learning experience. I went on field trips to observe different urban sites first-hand (Punggol, Tengah, Bidadari, Tampines...), talked to residents about their neighbourhood experiences, and applied systems thinking as a toolkit to formalise observations. The biggest takeaway among many is probably starting to appreciate cities as intricately connected urban systems. 


## What am I working on now?

- Various projects in machine learning, economic forecasting, and econometrics. 
- Learning about advanced macroeconomic theory. 
- Urban planning strategy and pro forma analysis of a hypothetical urban transformation project in Shanghai.


## What's next?

Realising I'm starting a new chapter of life soon after graduation is exciting but scary... I hope to work on something innovative and impactful as I progress.
